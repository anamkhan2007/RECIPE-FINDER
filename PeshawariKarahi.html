Peshawari Karahi

A bold and rustic Karahi made with minimal ingredients, full of traditional Peshawari flavor 🇵🇰

⏱️ Cooking Time

  • Preparation: 15 minutes
  • Cooking: 45–50 minutes

🍽️ Nutrition (Approx)

  • Calories: ~500 kcal per serving
  • Protein: ~35g
  • Fat: ~30g
  • Carbs: ~10g

🧂 Ingredients

  • 1 kg bone-in chicken or mutton
  • 3–4 large tomatoes (chopped or peeled)
  • 1 tbsp crushed ginger
  • 1 tbsp crushed garlic
  • 1/2 cup cooking oil or ghee
  • 1 tsp crushed black pepper
  • 1 tsp red chili flakes (adjust to taste)
  • Salt to taste
  • Fresh coriander (for garnish)
  • Julienned ginger (for garnish)
  • Green chilies (sliced, for garnish)

👨‍🍳 Instructions

  1. Heat oil or ghee in a heavy-bottomed wok (karahi).
  2. Add chicken/mutton and sear until the color changes and it starts to brown slightly.
  3. Add crushed garlic and ginger, sauté for a few minutes until fragrant.
  4. Add chopped tomatoes and salt. Cover and cook on medium flame until the tomatoes are soft and meat is nearly done.
  5. Remove the lid and cook on high flame to evaporate water and thicken the masala.
  6. Add chili flakes and crushed black pepper. Stir and fry the karahi for 5–10 minutes until oil separates.
  7. Garnish with green chilies, julienned ginger, and chopped coriander.
  8. Serve hot with naan or tandoori roti.
Tip: Authentic Peshawari Karahi avoids onions and uses only tomatoes and minimal spices to highlight the meat’s natural flavor. Cook on high heat at the end for the best taste.
