Sindhi Biryani

A spicy, layered, and aromatic rice dish from Sindhi cuisine

⏱️ Cooking Time

  • Prep Time: 20 minutes
  • Cook Time: 50 minutes
  • Total: ~1 hr 10 min

🍛 Nutrition (Per Serving)

  • Calories: 550–600 kcal
  • Protein: 28g
  • Fat: 20–25g
  • Carbs: 65–70g

🧂 Ingredients

For Chicken Marinade:

  • 1 kg chicken (bone-in or boneless)
  • 1 cup yogurt
  • 2 tbsp ginger-garlic paste
  • 2 tsp red chili powder
  • ½ tsp turmeric powder
  • 2 tsp coriander powder
  • 1 tsp garam masala
  • Salt to taste
  • 2 tbsp lemon juice

For Gravy & Layering:

  • 1 cup cooking oil
  • 3 medium onions (sliced)
  • 3 tomatoes (chopped)
  • 5–6 green chilies (split)
  • 2 medium potatoes (halved or quartered)
  • Fresh mint & coriander (chopped)
  • Kewra essence or saffron in milk

For Rice:

  • 750g Basmati rice (soaked 30 min)
  • Salt as needed
  • Whole spices (bay leaf, cardamom, cinnamon, cloves)

👩‍🍳 Instructions

  1. Mix chicken with marinade ingredients. Let sit for 30 minutes.
  2. Heat oil, fry sliced onions until golden. Remove half for garnish.
  3. Add chicken and sauté 10–15 mins until no longer pink.
  4. Add tomatoes, green chilies, and potatoes. Cook until tomatoes are soft and oil separates.
  5. Stir in chopped mint, coriander, and kewra or saffron milk. Let simmer briefly.
  6. Boil water in another pot with whole spices and salt. Add soaked rice and boil until 80% cooked. Drain immediately.
  7. Layer biryani: add chicken curry layer, then rice. Repeat layers. Top with fried onions, mint, and saffron.
  8. Cover and cook on high for 10 mins, then on low for 20 mins (for steam/dum).
  9. Let it rest for 10 mins. Fluff gently and serve hot with raita or salad.
Chef’s Tip: You can use mutton instead of chicken — just increase cooking time for the meat accordingly.
