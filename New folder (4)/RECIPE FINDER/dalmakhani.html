Dal Makhani

A rich, creamy North Indian lentil dish cooked with butter and spices

⏱️ Cooking Time

  • Prep Time: 10 minutes
  • Cook Time: 60–90 minutes
  • Total Time: ~1.5 hours

🍛 Nutrition (Per Serving)

  • Calories: 400–450 kcal
  • Protein: 18g
  • Fat: 22g
  • Carbs: 35g

🧂 Ingredients

  • 1 cup whole black lentils (sabut urad dal)
  • ¼ cup kidney beans (rajma)
  • 1 medium onion (finely chopped)
  • 2 tomatoes (pureed)
  • 1 tbsp ginger-garlic paste
  • 2 tbsp butter + 1 tbsp oil
  • 1 tsp cumin seeds
  • ½ tsp turmeric powder
  • 1 tsp red chili powder
  • 1 tsp coriander powder
  • ½ tsp garam masala
  • ½ cup fresh cream
  • Salt to taste
  • Fresh coriander for garnish

👩‍🍳 Instructions

  1. Soak urad dal and rajma overnight. Pressure cook them with salt until soft (4–5 whistles).
  2. In a pan, heat butter and oil. Add cumin seeds. Let them splutter.
  3. Sauté onions until golden, then add ginger-garlic paste. Cook until raw smell goes away.
  4. Add tomato puree, turmeric, chili, and coriander powder. Cook until oil separates.
  5. Add the boiled dal and rajma. Mix and simmer on low for 30–40 mins (stir occasionally).
  6. Add cream, garam masala, and a little more butter. Simmer for 5–10 more minutes.
  7. Garnish with fresh coriander and a swirl of cream.
  8. Serve hot with naan or steamed basmati rice.
Tip: The longer it simmers, the creamier it gets! Traditional dal makhani simmers for hours.
