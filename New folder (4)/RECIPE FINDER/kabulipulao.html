Kabuli Pulao

Aromatic Afghan rice dish with lamb, raisins, and carrots — rich and festive! 🇦🇫

⏱️ Cooking Time

  • Preparation: 20 minutes
  • Cooking: 1 hour 30 minutes

🍽️ Nutrition (Approx)

  • Calories: ~600 kcal per serving
  • Protein: ~30g
  • Fat: ~20g
  • Carbs: ~70g

🧂 Ingredients

  • 500g basmati rice (soaked)
  • 500g lamb or beef (bone-in preferred)
  • 2 medium onions (sliced)
  • 3-4 cloves garlic (crushed)
  • 1 tsp cumin seeds
  • 1 tsp ground cardamom
  • Salt to taste
  • 1/2 tsp black pepper
  • 3 carrots (julienned)
  • 1/2 cup raisins
  • 1/2 cup cooking oil or ghee
  • Water or stock as needed
  • Optional: slivered almonds for garnish

👨‍🍳 Instructions

  1. In a large pot, heat oil and sauté onions until golden brown.
  2. Add lamb/beef pieces, garlic, salt, pepper, cumin, and cardamom. Cook until meat browns.
  3. Add enough water to cover the meat. Cover and simmer until tender (about 45–60 minutes).
  4. In a separate pan, lightly fry julienned carrots in oil until soft. Add raisins and cook briefly until plump. Set aside.
  5. Once meat is cooked, remove and set aside. Strain the broth if needed.
  6. Boil soaked rice in the meat broth until 70% cooked. Drain excess water.
  7. In a large pot or oven-safe dish, layer rice with meat and some fried carrots/raisins.
  8. Cover tightly and steam on low heat (dum) for 20–25 minutes.
  9. Garnish with remaining carrots, raisins, and optional almonds before serving.
Tip: For deeper flavor, use lamb-on-bone and let it cook slowly. This dish is often served at weddings and festivals in Afghanistan.
