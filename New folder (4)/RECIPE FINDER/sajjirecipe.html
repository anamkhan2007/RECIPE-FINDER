Balochi Sajji

A traditional whole chicken roast from Balochistan, seasoned simply and slow-cooked

⏱️ Cooking Time

  • Prep Time: 15 minutes
  • Marination Time: 2–3 hours (recommended)
  • Cook Time: 60–90 minutes

🍽️ Nutrition (Per Serving)

  • Calories: ~400–500 kcal
  • Protein: 35g
  • Fat: 30g
  • Carbs: 2g

🧂 Ingredients

  • 1 whole chicken (about 1 to 1.5 kg)
  • 2 tbsp white vinegar or lemon juice
  • 1½ tsp salt (adjust to taste)
  • 1 tbsp crushed black pepper
  • 1 tbsp crushed coriander seeds (optional)
  • 1 tbsp oil or ghee (for basting)
  • Optional: green chili paste or cumin for extra flavor (non-traditional)

🔥 Instructions

  1. Clean and pat dry the whole chicken. Make light slits on the meat for flavor absorption.
  2. Mix salt, pepper, vinegar (or lemon juice) and rub all over the chicken — including inside.
  3. Marinate for 2–3 hours for deeper flavor (or overnight in fridge).
  4. Preheat oven to 180°C (350°F). Place chicken on a rack or hang in traditional sajji style.
  5. Bake/roast for 60–90 minutes, turning occasionally and basting with oil or ghee.
  6. Once skin is golden and crisp, remove and rest for 10 minutes before serving.
  7. Serve hot with naan, rice, or traditional Balochi rice and salad.
Tip: Traditional sajji uses *no turmeric or red chili*. The meat is slow-cooked near coals. For oven sajji, keep temperature moderate and rotate for even cooking.
