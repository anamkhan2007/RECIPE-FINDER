Makki Di Roti

A traditional Punjabi cornmeal flatbread served best with Sarson da Saag

üßÇ Ingredients

  • 2 cups Makki (cornmeal) flour
  • 1 tsp carom seeds (ajwain)
  • Salt to taste
  • Warm water (as needed to knead)
  • Ghee or butter (for cooking & serving)

üë©‚Äçüç≥ Instructions

  1. In a large bowl, mix makki flour, ajwain, and salt.
  2. Gradually add warm water and knead into a soft dough (it will be slightly crumbly).
  3. Divide the dough into equal portions and shape into balls.
  4. Flatten each ball gently between your palms or roll between two plastic sheets or parchment.
  5. Heat a tawa (griddle) on medium heat. Carefully transfer the roti onto the hot tawa.
  6. Cook on one side, then flip. Apply ghee and cook both sides until golden spots appear.
  7. Serve hot with butter and sarson da saag.
Tip: To make rolling easier, use a ziplock bag or two sheets of parchment paper. Always keep the dough warm and moist.
