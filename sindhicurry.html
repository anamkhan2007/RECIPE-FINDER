Sindhi Curry

A tangy, spicy gram flour-based curry from Sindhi cuisine

⏱️ Cooking Time

  • Prep Time: 15 minutes
  • Cook Time: 40 minutes
  • Total: ~55 minutes

🍛 Nutrition (Per Serving)

  • Calories: ~250 kcal
  • Protein: 8g
  • Fat: 8–10g
  • Carbs: 35g

🧂 Ingredients

  • ½ cup gram flour (besan)
  • 2 tbsp oil
  • 1 tsp cumin seeds
  • 1 tsp mustard seeds
  • 1 tsp fenugreek seeds
  • 8–10 curry leaves
  • 2–3 dried red chilies
  • 1 tsp turmeric powder
  • 1 tsp red chili powder
  • 2 tbsp tamarind pulp (or lemon juice)
  • 5 cups water
  • 2 medium drumsticks (vegetable, cut into pieces)
  • 1 medium eggplant (cubed)
  • 1 small potato (cubed)
  • 1 carrot (sliced)
  • Salt to taste
  • Fresh coriander for garnish

👩‍🍳 Instructions

  1. In a bowl, mix gram flour with water to make a smooth paste (no lumps).
  2. Heat oil in a large pot. Add cumin, mustard, fenugreek seeds, curry leaves, and dry red chilies. Let them splutter.
  3. Add gram flour paste and stir continuously to avoid lumps.
  4. Add 5 cups of water, turmeric, red chili powder, and salt. Bring to a boil.
  5. Add vegetables (drumsticks, eggplant, potato, carrot) and cook until vegetables are tender (about 20–25 minutes).
  6. Add tamarind pulp (or lemon juice) and simmer for 5–7 minutes.
  7. Garnish with fresh coriander and serve hot with steamed rice.
Chef’s Tip: For extra tanginess, add a few tomatoes or a teaspoon of amchur (dry mango powder) while boiling.
